Mickey D's is Officially Uncool!

I've been seeing a lot of McDoobie ads lately, and I've noticed an interesting phenomenon: rather than simply playing their jingle at the end, they have someone simply say "badabapbawhatever." They don't actually say whatever, but it's ethically acceptable for me to quote it as that because, as far as I can tell, the whole point in them doing it is to demonstrate that they are too cool to care about playing their jingle.

In more concrete terms, my hypothesis is as follows: in order to appeal to a modern audience, ManBoob is implementing a seemingly neo-dadaist strategy of "stripping away" a portion of their symbolism, specifically, the mode and melody of their jingle. I then postulate further that this makes McDiddler uncool. The entire point of strippage is to demonstrate what symbolism is or is not actually essential, and the only reason the jingle has any "non-essential" parts is because O'Hare controls burgers. What is intended to be a demonstration of cool modern chillness is in actuality a demonstration of the corporate dominance that Donald holds over the nation and world.

McDude, you may have avoided this scathing review if only my wandering eye had passed you over. You flew too close to the sun in wilfully advertising in The Valley, known In-N-Out Burger territory! Something was bound to break.
