Fragments of an Ongoing Meno Speedrun

Each fragment contains approximately two to three small manuscripts worth of hard-core Ancient Greek Socratic Dialogue! Many early fragments have been lost but will be published upon their having been found. New fragments published biweekly!

2024-02-27

ΣΩΚ
Some great good fortune I seem to have experienced indeed, ΜΕΝΟ, if while seeking one virtue I have found some swarm of bees lying down beside you. But nevertheless, ΜΕΝΟ, according to this form regarding the swarm of bees, if you were being asked about whatever the being of the bee is, [and] you were saying they are many and of every kind, how would you separate it for me, if to you I asked: "Do you say because of this they are many and of every kind and differing from each other, namely because of their being bees? Or maybe not differing at all because of this, but because of another, for example maybe from beauty or from size or another thing of these sorts?" Tell me, what would you separate out of this question?
ΜΕΝ
I would say this for my part, namely that not at all are they differing, by which bees are, the one from another.
ΣΩΚ
Then if I had spoken next of these: "Let me tell you now to say this for me, ΜΕΝΟ: because of what are they all not at all differing but the same, what do you say this to be?" Perhaps you would be able to say what for me?
ΜΕΝ
For my part.
ΣΩΚ
And just so about the virtues: even if they are many and of all kinds, they quite all indeed have some one same form on account of they are virtues, and at which [good anywhere it is] ... [to the answering for the] ... [that to have shown], he happens upon the being of virtue: or do you not understand what I say?
ΜΕΝ
I think indeed for me I understand: however not as I wish indeed yet I do grasp what is being asked.
ΣΩΚ
[...]
ΜΕΝ
For me I think of health at least to be the same both of man and of woman.
ΣΩΚ
[...]
ΜΕΝ
Not for myself.
ΣΩΚ
And will virtue taken to be in regard to virtue differ at all, whether in a child or in an old man, whether in a woman or in a man.
ΜΕΝ
For myself I somehow think, ΣΩΚΡΑΤΕΣ, that this is no more like those others.

2024-03-01

ΣΩΚ
What? Were you not saying virtue of man is to manage a city well but of woman, a house?
ΜΕΝ
I indeed.
ΣΩΚ
And is it possible to manage well either a city or a house or any other whatsoever if not managing temperately and justly?
ΜΕΝ
Certainly not.
ΣΩΚ
Therefore whoever justly and temperately manages, with justice and temperance they will manage?
ΜΕΝ
It is necessary.
ΣΩΚ
So both need the same, if indeed they intend to be good, both the woman and the man, of justice and of temperance.
ΜΕΝ
So it appears.
ΣΩΚ
What about the child and old man? But surely, being intemperant and unjust, good would not come into being?
ΜΕΝ
Certainly not.
ΣΩΚ
But if temperate and just?
ΜΕΝ
Yes.
ΣΩΚ
All men then are good in the same manner; for in attaining the same they become good.
ΜΕΝ
So it seems.
ΣΩΚ
Perhaps, if the same virtue were not the same, they would not be good in the same manner.
ΜΕΝ
Certainly not.
ΣΩΚ
Now that you see it is the same virtue of all, try to say and remember what ΓΟΡΓΙΑΣ and you along with that say it to be.
ΜΕΝ
What else is it indeed than something like ruling men; if you really do seek one thing against all.
ΣΩΚ
Well I certainly seek indeed. But is it the same virtue both of child, ΜΕΝΟ, and of slave, only to be ruling his master, and does it seem to you the ruler would be a slave?
ΜΕΝ
Not at all to me it seems, ΣΩΚΡΑΤΕΣ.
ΣΩΚ
Not likely, Best One; and yet here keep looking. You say to be ruling such as anything. Should I not add to this "decently," and "not of injustice"?
ΜΕΝ
I anticipate so for my part; for justice, ΣΩΚΡΑΤΕΣ, is virtue.
ΣΩΚ
Which: virtue, ΜΕΝΟ, or a certain virtue?
ΜΕΝ
What do you mean by this?

2024-03-05

ΣΩΚ
Like as about another of whatever. For example, if you mean what I would have said about roundness for my part that it is some figure, thus not simply that it is a figure. For and thus this I would have said, that also there are others of figures.
ΜΕΝ
You are indeed speaking correctly, since also I myself say not only justice but also others to be virtues.
ΣΩΚ
What are these? Tell me. For example also I myself would have told you of other figures, if you ordered me: and you then should tell me other virtues.
ΜΕΝ
Well Courage for me seems to be virtue and Temperance and Wisdom and Magnificence and very many others.
ΣΩΚ
Again, ΜΕΝΟ, we have suffered the same thing: again we have found many virtues [when we seek] one, in a different manner than just now: but the one, which is in the midst of all of these, we are unable to discover.
ΜΕΝ
For I am not yet able, ΣΩΚΡΑΤΕΣ, as you seek, to have taken one virtue over all, as in for the others.
ΣΩΚ
Reasonably so: but I myself will be eager, if I am able, to have moved us forward. For I suppose that to learn is just so about the whole: if someone should ask you what I myself was just now saying,"What is shape, ΜΕΝΟ?" If you had for it said roundness, and if they had for you said, "Which is roundness - shape or some shape?" Perhaps you would have said some shape.
ΜΕΝ
Quite so!
ΣΩΚ
Therefore for nothing, that also there are others of shapes?
ΜΕΝ
Yes.
ΣΩΚ
And if indeed he were asking you what sort or kind, would you have said it?
ΜΕΝ
For my part.
ΣΩΚ
And again if about color in the same manner he had asked what thing it is, and upon your saying that it is the light, after this he took up the one question: "Which is light - color or some color?" you would have said some color, because also others happen to exist.
ΜΕΝ
For my part.
ΣΩΚ
And if indeed he was urging you to say other colors, you would have said others, which happen to be colors in no lesser way than light?
ΜΕΝ
Yes.
