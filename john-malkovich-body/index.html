Being John Malkovich as Body Horror

Being John Malkovich is generally regarded as a film about people who discover a door that leads into John Malkovich's head and the emotional impacts it has on them. I propose, however, that the film is really a body horror film, presented from the perspective of the monster.

Consider John Carpenter's film The Thing: an alien takes control of your body such that nobody can tell it isn't you — when really, your mind is long gone, or at least inaccessible. The horror of this film is multifaceted, but I'm sure many would agree that a large driving factor is the fear of somehow losing control of your body.

Peter Watts short story The Things reverses this plot, showing us the same story from the perspective of the thing. In it, we see how humans are incapable of sympathy for the thing and what it can offer.

I propose that Being John Malkovich is a horror film of the same category as The Things. We are tricked by perspective into sympathizing with the monsters — Craig, Lotte, and Maxine, as well as Dr. Lester and his cronies — as they take over Malkovich's body.

I propose, additionally, for the sake of completeness, that a sequel, The Beings in John Malkovich, should be published, telling the same story from the perspective of John Malkovich.
