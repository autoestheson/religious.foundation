I Love the Whole World!

For me, the key to being happy is a positive outlook filled with gratitude for the world. Epictetus agrees: he writes in On Freedom that we cannot control anything but ourselves, so we should be grateful for our continued existence.

With that in mind, I bring up the Discovery Channel's 2008 advertisement campaign, I Love the Whole World, involving a short, catchy melody featuring many people singing about what they love about the world.

I don't actually care about its lyrics; I care about its structure: each verse consists of several lines of "I love {X}", where X is either a 3 syllable phrase falling in pitch or a 4 syllable phrase rising in pitch, followed by a short chorus.

On a side note, as I understand it, this sort of lyrical improvisation is how epic poems were recited: rather than memorize every single word, a rhapsode would memorize formulae used to express a general idea more specifically.

I don't mean this to say that I am composing an epic poem — I neither wrote the structure, nor is it particularly epic. But I do consider myself in this regard to be a rhapsode continuing on an ancient tradition for a beautiful purpose.
