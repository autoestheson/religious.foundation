Clouds are Awesome, Terrible Things

As a born & raised Valley Dude, as in, from That Valley Without Weather, I have had limited experience with, well, weather. So it's been a bit of a shock to arrive to university in the middle of what is called "monsoon season" — where every day brings rain. Hell, I spent my first week backpacking in The Wilderness, where I daily risked thunderstorms while almost completely exposed! What a shock to my system.

In the Valley, clouds were largely meaningless. It was either sunny or cloudy, with the difference being purely ornamental.

Here, I have discovered of the meaning of clouds. While studying outside, I'm able to lose myself in the clouds, whether over me or else some far off mountain. I watch in wonder as they grow, dance, and mesh together across the sky. I watch in awe as they grow dark and heavy, as in the distance they appear to extend tendrils of water to the ground, and as that rain reaches me.

All that that is, there is still one thing more: at the end of it all, when on top of my hill I can see a rainbow stretch across the entire sky. Being this intimate with the sky helps me to understand how the Greeks so revered and feared Zeus, God of the Sky.
